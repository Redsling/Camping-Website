Camping method

This is page will help you have a pleasant camping experience and give you ideas on what you can do when you come to our camping grounds.

1. Tent Camping

Tent camping is the most basic type of camping that everyone essentially knows about — it involves pitching a tent at a local park, recreational spots or just about anywhere as long as it is the open outdoors. Tent camping has come a long way but the very basics of it involve spending a few nights in nature, enjoying food over a bonfire or your own camping set up and endless stargazing. Tent camping can include a variety of styles in shared spaces, with the idea of spending a night outdoors! There are not a lot of specific descriptions to determine the ideal setup.

2. Backpacking/Hiking Camping

Backpacking would often mean just roughing it out and finding ways to live out of your backpack with a mix of homestays and sometimes camping in the mountains. However, camping is very much a mainstay in the backpacking world as more people dig the adventure lifestyle and stay away from the beaten path!

3. Car Camping

Options like car camping are the perfect introductory way to camping in general. All you need is to embark on a road trip or look up national parks where camping is allowed and set up camp at that spot. You'll have access to camping sites that include showers or communal areas. These are places where you can hang out with other people or have some level of comfort without forsaking all your necessities! It is also an excellent way to introduce camping to your kids. You can use it as a way to have some adventure without venturing far out in the woods. The plusses include protection from bad weather where waiting it out doesn't seem like a bad idea after all if Plan A doesn't work out!

4. RV/Van Camping

If vanlife has inspired you to camp, then you'll notice how Instagram has taken off with a variety of gram-worthy layouts! It's not all roses and fairies, as it does require a fair bit of setup and planning. Campervans are a less decked up version of recreational vehicles (RV) and offer the minimalist version of being on the road. RVs, on the other hand, are great if you're looking for a long-term setup or need more amenities while on the road. With thousands of excursions these days offering a mix of vanlife and campervan/RV experience, you could always sign up for these tasters before seeing if a complete lifestyle overhaul is for you!
